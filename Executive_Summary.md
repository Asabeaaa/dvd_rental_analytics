# Executive Summary

## Business Context
The business operates two physical DVD rental stores, each carrying a number of films across multiple categories. Customers register at a store, browse available titles, and take films home for a set rental period. Payment is collected when the film is returned. If a customer returns a film past its due date, a late fee applies on top of the base rental charge.

The data records who rented what, from which store, when it was due, and whether payment was received. Over time this data tells us how the business is performing in terms of the value we get from clients, which films clients are renting the most, the general store performance and so on. This helps us make executive decisions on what to restock and how to capitalize backed by facts.

The analytical challenge is to transform raw company data into business intelligence that supports decision-making across customer retention, revenue growth, and operational efficiency.

## Analytical Objectives

**1. Understand the current state of the business.**
Before any improvement can be made, the business needs a clear picture of where it stands. This covers revenue performance across categories and time, how each store is performing, and how efficiently the inventory is being used. Understanding the baseline is what makes it possible to identify what is already working, what to change and where to invest.

**2. Understand customer behaviour and value.**
Customers differ significantly in how often they rent, how much they spend, and how long they remain active. Segmenting customers and calculating lifetime value allows the business to identify its most valuable customers. For profitability, understanding how much value we get from clients allows us to understand how much money and effort should go into acquiring and retaining said clients.

**3. Optimise revenue across film categories.**
Not all categories generate equal revenue, and high rental volume does not always translate to high earnings. Understanding category performance across both how often people rent and revenue generated reveals pricing gaps and demand patterns the business can act on. These demand patterns could also be seasonal or driven by pricing.

**4. Improve inventory and store operations.**
Copies of films sit idle while others are constantly in demand. Identifying overstocked and understocked titles alongside store-level performance differences helps the business redistribute stock and understand what drives location performance.


## Analytical Approach

The data is validated and explored before building purpose-built datasets for each area of analysis. Customer, revenue, and inventory themes are analysed independently, with a predictive model layered on top to flag churn risk. Reports are then combined to create a dashboard that brings the key metrics together in one place.

## Analytical Questions

### Customer Intelligence

1. Which customers generate the most revenue over their lifetime?
2. Which customers are at risk of churning based on recency of rental activity, and how does this risk distribute across the customer base?

### Revenue Optimization

3. Which film categories generate the most revenue, and do the highest-earning categories also have the highest rental volume or are there gaps between popularity and earnings?
4. Are there seasonal patterns in category demand?

### Inventory and Operations

5. Which films have the highest turnover rate relative to their copy count, and which are overstocked with low demand?
6. Which store generates more revenue and processes more rentals, and does the category mix at each store explain the performance difference?

### Innovation and Custom Analysis

7. Can customer churn be predicted from behavioural features such as rental frequency, payment rate, and lifetime value, and which features carry the most predictive weight?
8. How can identifying customers at risk of churning inform targeted marketing efforts, and which customer segments should be prioritised for retention campaigns?
